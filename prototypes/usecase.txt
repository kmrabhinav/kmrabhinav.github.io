1) Governance at a Glance
Purpose: Ensure AI use cases are safe, compliant, clinically appropriate, technically robust, ethically sound, and financially viable before build/buy decisions and go live.
Scope: All AI/ML use cases (generative + predictive), including POCs, pilots, shadow IT, vendor demos with real data, and embedded AI in SaaS.
Decision Bodies:
•	Risk Stripe Reviewers (8 functional reviewers): Risk, Cyber, Tech Architecture, Privacy, Compliance, Clinical, Legal, Finance.
•	Responsible AI Committee (RAIC): Voting body for final approve/decline/exception decisions, informed by stripe inputs.
•	Executive Sponsor (per use case): Accountable owner for outcomes and risk remediation.
Principles:
•	Proportionality (risk-tiered rigor)
•	Explainability & Traceability
•	Defense-in-Depth (controls + monitoring)
•	Human Oversight
•	Clinical Safety-first
•	Privacy-by-Design
•	Continuous Monitoring (post go live)

2) End-to-End Workflow (Automated in Appian)
Stage 0: Intake
•	Trigger: Business/Tech submits a use case via Appian Intake Form.
•	Auto-classification: Assign a Risk Tier (Low/Med/High/Critical) using embedded scoring (see §7).
•	Auto-routing: Appian spawns parallel review tasks to all 8 stripes with SLA timers and reminders.
•	Artifacts required at Intake:
o	Problem statement, intended users, expected outcomes/benefits
o	Population impacted, clinical touchpoints (if any)
o	Data categories (PHI/PII/sensitive), sources, and residency
o	Model type (genAI, predictive, RAG, rules + ML, vendor API)
o	Deployment pattern (on prem, VPC, SaaS), integrations
o	Human-in-the-loop points and override controls
o	Evaluation plan (accuracy, bias, safety, clinical validation)
o	Explainability method, logging/monitoring plan
o	Vendor details (if applicable): SOC 2, ISO 27001, SBOM, BAAs, HIPAA posture, support & SLAs
o	Financials and business case (TCO, ROI, sensitivity)
o	Go-live target date and pilot plans (if any)
Stage 1: Stripe Reviews (Parallel)
Each stripe receives a task with a structured checklist and must choose:
Approve | Decline | Need More Info | Approve with Exceptions (Pilot or Conditional)
Each decision must include rationale, control requirements, and evidence. Appian enforces mandatory fields.
SLAs (business days):
•	Low: 5; Medium: 10; High: 15; Critical: 20
(Clock pauses on “Need More Info”. Escalations to RAIC Chair at SLA-1.)
Stage 2: Pre-RAIC Synthesis
•	Appian compiles stripe decisions into a Use Case Risk Dossier: risk summary, control gaps, exception requests, pilot guardrails, and scoring.
•	Conflicts flagged (e.g., Legal=Decline vs. Tech=Approve).
•	RAIC agenda item created automatically; calendar invite + briefing pack sent.
Stage 3: RAIC Review & Vote
•	Participants: Voting members from Risk, Cyber, Architecture, Privacy, Compliance, Clinical, Legal, Finance; non voting: Use Case Owner, Product, Data Science lead, Vendor (optional).
•	Quorum: ≥ 75% voting members present; Clinical mandatory for any clinically adjacent use case.
•	Voting Options:
o	Approve (unconditional)
o	Approve with Conditions (controls & deadlines)
o	Limited Pilot with Exceptions (strict guardrails)
o	Defer – Need More Info
o	Decline (with rationale)
•	Decision Log: Appian captures vote by member, rationales, conditions, target dates, and responsible owners. PDF minutes auto generated and archived.
Stage 4: Orchestration & Tracking
•	Appian auto-creates Jira Align epics/stories for remediation items and control implementation; links to the use case record.
•	Go Live Gate in Appian checks all required controls are met (policy, security, clinical validation, contracts, financial sign off).
•	Post Go Live Continuous Monitoring tasks scheduled (KPIs/KRIs, drift, incidents, audit logs).

3) Roles & Responsibilities (RACI)
Role	Responsibilities
Use Case Owner (A/R)	Intake, requirements, explainability narrative, evaluation plan, pilot plan, business value.
Risk	Model risk classification, risk aggregation, control strategy, residual risk statement.
Cybersecurity	Threat modeling, SaaS/Vendor security posture, data-in-transit/at-rest controls, key mgmt, IAM, token security for genAI.
Tech Architecture	Reference architecture fit, integration patterns, data residency, scalability/HA/DR, observability.
Privacy	HIPAA/PHI, de identification, DUA/BAA requirements, data minimization, retention.
Compliance	Regulatory mapping (HIPAA, CMS, state laws), policy conformance, audit readiness.
Clinical	Clinical safety, efficacy, validation design, clinician oversight, documentation, no patient harm assurance.
Legal	Contracts, IP, indemnity, liability caps, vendor terms, DPAs, BAAs, model output ownership; marketing/claims review.
Finance	TCO/ROI, sensitivity analysis, contingent liabilities (e.g., penalties), budget alignment.
RAIC Chair (A)	Agenda, quorum, voting, minutes, conflict resolution.
Risk Office (R)	Framework upkeep, metrics, training, policy updates.
(A=Accountable, R=Responsible)

4) Stripe-Specific Review Checklists (must be captured in Appian forms)
4.1 Risk
•	Risk tiering score; model risk class (e.g., Safety Critical vs. Advisory).
•	Bias/harm assessment plan; red team test plan (for genAI).
•	Residual risk and compensating controls.
•	KRIs & thresholds (e.g., error rates, false positives/negatives, clinical escalation rates).
4.2 Cyber
•	Data flow diagram; network boundaries; egress controls for LLM calls.
•	Secrets management; key vault; token handling; rate limiting; prompt injection defenses.
•	Vendor security: SOC2/ISO, pen test, vuln mgmt, SBOM, SSO/SAML, PAM, logging.
•	GenAI specific: output filtering, content moderation, retrieval isolation (RAG), isolation of tenant data.
4.3 Tech Architecture
•	Alignment to reference architectures; DR/HA, performance SLOs.
•	Observability: structured logging, traces, model telemetry, versioning, feature store governance.
•	Model lifecycle: CI/CD for ML (MLOps), rollbacks, shadow/blue green deploy.
4.4 Privacy
•	Data classification (PHI/PII); de identification method; differential privacy (if applicable).
•	Data locality/residency; retention schedule; DSR handling (access, deletion).
•	Legal bases/consent; minimum necessary; privacy impact assessment (PIA).
4.5 Compliance
•	Regulatory mapping (HIPAA/CMS/HITECH/state); audit controls and evidencing plan.
•	Human in the loop where mandated; documentation pack (SOPs, training, quick guides).
4.6 Clinical
•	Clinical validation protocol (prospective/retrospective), performance thresholds, non inferiority margins.
•	Harm taxonomy, safety incident triage, escalation to clinical governance.
•	Explainability suitable for clinicians; overrides/second opinion path.
4.7 Legal
•	Contractuals: IP ownership of outputs, indemnities, limitation of liability.
•	BAA/DPA terms; data use limits; right to audit; sub processors.
•	Claims review: avoid over promising clinical impact; marketing & label compliance.
4.8 Finance
•	TCO (build vs. buy), cloud costs, usage-based LLM costs, sensitivity.
•	ROI with time to value; payback; benefit attribution.
•	Budget owner acknowledgement.

5) Decision Types & Required Explanations
Approve (Unconditional):
•	All stripes “Approve”; residual risks documented as acceptable; controls in place.
Approve with Conditions:
•	Specific controls with owners and due dates (e.g., “Add prompt injection filter before GA”).
•	Appian gates go live until pre conditions met; post conditions tracked via Jira Align.
Pilot with Exceptions:
•	Temporary exceptions allowed with strict guardrails (see §6).
•	Time boxed (e.g., ≤ 90 days), limited scope/users/data; no production PHI unless approved by Privacy & Clinical (if applicable).
•	Exit criteria defined upfront.
Need More Info:
•	Appian auto cycles to owner with a consolidated RFI and single resubmission path to reduce thrash.
Decline:
•	Document clear rationale: unacceptable clinical risk, privacy non compliance, adverse legal terms, infeasible architecture, negative NPV, etc.
•	Provide remediation guidance (what would need to change).

6) Pilot Guardrails (for Exceptions)
•	Data: Prefer de identified/synthetic; if PHI needed, justify and minimize; access logging mandatory.
•	Users: Limited to trained internal users; audit of access; no patient facing without Clinical sign off.
•	Scope: Narrow use case, constrained prompts/tasks; block high risk capabilities.
•	Controls: Moderation, toxicity filters, jailbreak prevention, retrieval isolation, watermarking/labeling of AI generated content.
•	Human Oversight: Mandatory review/approval for high impact outputs.
•	Telemetry: Capture prompts, outputs, user actions, and model versions.
•	Incident Response: Clear path for reporting harmful outputs or clinical concerns within 24 hours.
•	Exit Criteria: Measurable thresholds (e.g., accuracy ≥ X, adverse event rate ≤ Y, latency ≤ Z); go/no go at RAIC.

7) Risk Tiering & Scoring (used for routing rigor)
Dimensions (0–5 each, weighted):
•	Clinical Impact (25%)
•	Privacy Sensitivity (20%)
•	Regulatory/Compliance Exposure (15%)
•	Security Threat Surface (15%)
•	Model/Technology Novelty (10%)
•	Scale/User Reach (10%)
•	Financial Exposure (5%)
Tiers:
•	Low (≤1.5): Streamlined review, standard controls.
•	Medium (1.6–2.5): Full stripe review.
•	High (2.6–3.5): Full review + RAIC vote required.
•	Critical (>3.5): RAIC + Executive Sponsor sign off; potential external validation.
Appian can auto compute score and display which reviewers are mandatory by tier (e.g., Clinical always mandatory when Clinical Impact ≥ 2).

8) Committee Charter (RAIC)
•	Mandate: Assure responsible AI outcomes; approve/deny exceptions; resolve cross stripe conflicts.
•	Membership: Senior reps from the 8 stripes (voting), plus Chair (voting), plus CIO or delegate (voting). Data Science Head and Product are non voting advisors.
•	Quorum: ≥ 75% of voting members; Clinical present when applicable.
•	Meeting Cadence: Bi weekly; ad hoc for critical items.
•	Voting: Simple majority; in tie, Chair escalates to CIO.
•	Records: Minutes, decisions, rationales, conditions, and votes archived in Appian; retention ≥ 7 years.
•	Conflicts of Interest: Declared at start; alternates assigned if needed.

11) Controls Checklist (Go Live Gate)
•	Policy & Training: Responsible AI policy acknowledged; role-specific training completed.
•	Data & Privacy: Data mapped; PHI/PII minimization; retention configured; DPA/BAA executed.
•	Security: IAM & RBAC enforced; encryption; secrets in vault; logging & SIEM forwarding; prompt/response moderation for genAI.
•	Model Ops: Versioned artifacts; evaluation results ≥ thresholds; rollback plan; canary/shadow test passed.
•	Clinical: Validation report; clinician sign off; incident escalation defined.
•	Legal/Compliance: Approvals complete; labeling/UX disclaimers; audit plan.
•	Finance: Funding confirmed; cost monitoring dashboards in place.
________________________________________
12) Post Go Live Monitoring & Triggers
•	Metrics: Accuracy, bias, drift, hallucination rate, latency, user override rate, clinical adverse events, privacy incidents, cost per transaction.
•	Alerts/Thresholds: Auto open incident in Appian on breach; freeze or rollback if critical thresholds triggered.
•	Periodic Review: Quarterly risk attestation; annual full re certification; sooner on material model/data change.

13) Success Metrics for the Program
•	Cycle Time: Intake → RAIC decision median days by tier.
•	Quality: % decisions with complete rationales; % controls met before go live.
•	Risk Outcomes: Incident rate per 1k actions; bias delta over time; privacy/security findings.
•	Adoption & Value: # use cases approved; ROI realized vs. forecast; clinician satisfaction for clinical use cases.
